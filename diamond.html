Other Characteristics

Fluorescence

  • Visible light emission when exposed to certian amount of UV
  • Levels - None, Very Slight, Slight, Faint, Medium, Strong, Very Strong

Polish

  • Surface smoothness
  • Levels - Ideal, Excellent, Very Good, Good, Fair and Poor

Symmetry

  • Exactness of the shape, arrangement and placement of the facets
  • Facets are small surfaces polished to a glassy smoothnesss
  • Ranks: Ideal, Excellent, Very Good, Good, Fair and Poor

Color Shade

  • Color Shade
  • Levels - None, White, Mixed, Yellow, Brown, Green, Grey, Black, Pink, Blue, Faint Brown, Faint Green, Other

Measurement Parameters

Lenth/Max (mm)

  • From Top to bottom. "Max" for round diamonds

Width/Min (mm)

  • Perpendicular to length. "Min" for round Diamonds

Depth (mm)

  • Distance between table and culet

Depth (%)

  • Depth / Width * 100

L/W Ratio (Length/Width Ratio, Ratio)

  • Length / Width. Commonly used for fancy shapes

Girdle (Range)

  • Space between crown and pavilion
  • Levels - Extremely Thin, Very Thin, Thin, Slightly Thin, Medium, Slightly Thick, Thick, Very Thick, Extremely Thick

Girdle Condition

  • Polished, Faceted, Bruted

Culet (mm)

  • Below Pavilion. Can be a point or small facet
  • Levels - None, Very Small, Small, Medium, Slightly Large, Large, Very Large, Extremely Large

Table (mm)

  • Top, flat, parallel to girdle

Table (%)

  • Table Width / Width * 100

Crown Angle (deg)

  • Number of degrees between the plane of the table and the bezel facets (lower crown facets)
  • Determines amount of light reflected into the pavilion at an angle which allows it to be reflected through the crown

Crown Height (mm)

  • Distance between girdle edge and table

Crown Height (%)

  • Crown Height / Average Diameter * 100

Pavilion Angle (deg)

  • Number of degrees between the plane of the table and the pavilion facets
  • Determines amount of the light travelling into the lower part of the diamond which reflects through the crown

Pavilion Depth (%)

  • Pavilion Depth / Girdle Diameter * 100

Diameter(mm)

  • Girdle diameter

    Level Description
    Visible Inclusion Visible
    EC1 Inclusion is not visible
    EC2 Medium inclusion is Visible (25% - 49%)
    EC3 Medium inclusion is Visible (50% - 74%)
Milky

  • Cloudy look. Occurs at levels of diamond processing. Range is 0 to 4

    Level Description
    0 non-existent
    4 most opaque or cloudy
    1-2 difficult to identify without a benchmark or trained eyes
Heart & Arrow
  • Precision-cut round, Exact angles and symmetry
  • Shows hearts and arrows pattern when viewed through a special tool
    • Arrows are visible from the top of the diamond
    • Hearts are visible when the diamond is face-down
  • Heart & Arrow can be graded in different categories such as,
    • EX: Extreme, VG: Very Good, NV: None Visible, N: None
